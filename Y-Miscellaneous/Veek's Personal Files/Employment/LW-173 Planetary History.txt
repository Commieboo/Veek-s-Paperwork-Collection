The size-14 warm Terran planet designated LW-173 was discovered approximately 190 years ago. Permanent, uncloaked orbital observation was authorized 177 years ago under CentCom approval.

At the time of discovery, the planet’s sentient species was predominantly nomadic, organized into clan-based societies. These clans migrated across vast territories using large mammalian trekking beasts, periodically converging at outposts and agricultural settlements. Their technological development was initially assessed as equivalent to the Terran “Roman era.” Based on this classification, cloaking of the observation post was deemed unnecessary.

However, this assessment proved incomplete. The species’ technological progression had advanced irregularly in certain domains with primitive steam applications and sophisticated celestial observation capabilities which had emerged significantly earlier than expected for their overall development level due to environmental and religious factors.

Approximately 163 years ago, the orbital outpost was detected by the native population. Its discovery had a profound and destabilizing effect on planetary society, triggering a period now classified as the Nomad Wars. Competing interpretations of the outpost’s meaning led to widespread conflict between clans. During this era, a more rigid feudal structure emerged, with weaker clans swearing fealty to stronger ones for protection and legitimacy.

In response to escalating instability, CentCom authorized direct intervention by Nanotrasen Corporate 146 years ago. The stated objectives were to uplift the native species and integration of the planet into the wider CentCom alliance.

This intervention intensified existing divisions. Some clans aligned themselves with Nanotrasen, gaining access to advanced technology and political leverage, while others resisted what they viewed as foreign domination. The result was a prolonged period of asymmetric conflict between pro- and anti-intervention factions.

Nanotrasen proceeded to accelerate technological development across aligned regions, elevating cooperative clans into positions of authority and gradually integrating the planet into the broader CentCom economic and political framework.

In the present day, LW-173 functions as a minor industrial hub. However, insurgent movements persist, advocating for a return to pre-contact social and religious structures, which have largely been displaced by imposed systems of governance and industry.
